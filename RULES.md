# 1. Card Anatomy
Every crocodile warrior (Crok) card has three core components that dictate how it performs in battle:

Faction / Group: Located at the top or indicated by the card's theme (e.g., Pirates, Dragons, Gladiators, Romans, Barbarians).

Combat Stats: Three values ranging from 1 to 9 printed at the bottom:

Strength (STR): Raw physical power.

Intelligence (INT): Tactical and cunning maneuvers.

Reflex (REF): Speed, agility, and dodging.

Special Ability: Text box describing a unique effect that can alter the rules or boost stats during combat.

# 2. Objective & How to Win
   The main goal of the game is to defeat your opponent's cards and claim them as trophies.

Official Win Condition: The first player to collect winning cards from 6 different factions wins the game.

Playground House Rule (Alternative): Many players preferred a harder variant where you had to collect 6 cards from the same faction to win, making deck-building much more strategic.

# 3. Game Setup
Each player builds a deck of at least 20 cards.

Shuffle your deck and draw a starting hand (usually 4–5 cards, depending on playground preference).

Decide who goes first (the Attacker) via rock-paper-scissors or a coin toss.

# 4. The Turn Structure (The Battle)
A standard round follows these four distinct phases:

Phase 1: The Attack
The Attacker chooses one Crok card from their hand, places it onto the battlefield (either face-down for suspense or face-up), and declares the combat stat for the round.

Example: "I attack with Strength!"

Phase 2: The Defense
The Defender chooses a card from their hand and plays it face-down onto the field, trying to guess or counter the Attacker's move.

Phase 3: The Reveal & Abilities
Both players flip their cards face-up. Special Abilities are triggered at this moment.

Abilities might add bonus points (e.g., "+2 to Reflex").

Some abilities change the combat type entirely (e.g., Gladiator Crok might force the battle to change from Intelligence to Strength).

Other cards can negate the opponent's ability completely.

Phase 4: Scoring & Next Turn
Players compare the final, modified values of the chosen stat.

The player with the higher value wins the round.

The winner takes the defeated opponent's card and places it face-up in front of them as a Győzelmi Pont (Victory Point).

The winner's own card goes to their discard pile.

The winner of the round becomes the Attacker for the next round.

# 5. The "Blind Battle" (Vakharc) - Tie Breaker
If the final values are a dead tie after calculating all stats and card abilities, a Blind Battle is triggered.

The Trap: The tying cards stay in the middle of the table. They are frozen in combat.

Blind Choice: Both players choose a new card from their hand and place it face-down on the table.

The Reveal: Both players flip their new cards simultaneously.

The Resolution: You compare the exact same attribute that started the tie (if the initial battle was Strength, the Blind Battle is also decided by Strength).

The Ultimate Prize: The winner of the Blind Battle takes all cards currently on the table (both the original tying cards and the new ones), scoring a massive advantage toward the 6-card victory condition.
